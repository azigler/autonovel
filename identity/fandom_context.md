# Fandom Context

## Fandom
Baldur's Gate 3 (BG3) by Larian Studios. Video game (CRPG), released August 2023. Based on Dungeons & Dragons 5th Edition, set in the Forgotten Realms. Canon is complete -- no DLC or expansions planned. The game has multiple endings per companion, which means fanfic can choose which ending to treat as "canon" for a given story.

## Canon Summary

The player character (referred to as "Tav" by the fandom, short for the default name suggestion) is infected with a mind flayer tadpole alongside several companions. The game follows their journey from the crashed nautiloid through the Wilderness, the Shadow-Cursed Lands, and into Baldur's Gate itself, culminating in a confrontation with the Netherbrain.

**What matters for fanfic:**

The companion characters are the heart of the game and the fandom. Each has a personal quest that functions as a complete character arc:

- **Astarion** was a magistrate turned vampire spawn, enslaved for 200 years by Cazador Szarr. His arc is about autonomy, trust, and whether power is the same as freedom. He can either ascend to full vampirism (gaining power, losing his soul) or reject ascension (remaining vulnerable but free). The fandom overwhelmingly prefers the "spawn" ending. His relationship with the tadpole -- something else controlling his body -- mirrors his history with Cazador.

- **Shadowheart** is a Sharran half-elf cleric carrying a mysterious artifact. Her arc is about faith, identity, and whether she can survive learning that everything she believes is a lie. She can choose Shar or Selune; the fandom leans Selune. Her defensiveness is armor over a deep well of grief.

- **Karlach** is a tiefling barbarian with an infernal engine for a heart. She spent ten years fighting in Avernus. Her arc is about joy, mortality, and touch -- her engine burns anyone who gets close. She is the emotional center of many party dynamics.

- **Gale** is a wizard who ate a Netherese orb to impress a goddess. His arc is about ambition, self-worth, and learning that he doesn't need to be exceptional to be loved.

- **Wyll** is a warlock who made a pact to save others. His arc is about sacrifice, identity (he's transformed into a devil), and the cost of heroism.

- **Lae'zel** is a githyanki warrior whose entire worldview is built on lies told by her queen. Her arc is about confronting the foundations of your identity.

**Emotional landscape the fandom cares about:** Camp scenes. Long rests where characters talk, argue, confess, and sit with each other. The game's most beloved moments are quiet ones -- Astarion watching the sunrise, Karlach's first hug, Shadowheart cutting her hair. Fanfic lives in these interstitial spaces.

**The tadpole** functions as a metaphor for violation, control, and intimacy. Using dream-visitor powers means using something parasitic for connection. This is rich thematic territory.

## Character Voices

### Astarion
- **Speech pattern:** Performative, theatrical, deflective. Uses wit as a shield. Sentences are often constructed to land a punchline or provoke a reaction. When genuine, he becomes shorter, less ornate -- the performance drops.
- **Vocabulary:** Educated, slightly archaic ("darling," "dear," "my sweet"). 200 years old but culturally frozen in his pre-spawn era. Uses endearments as both weapons and genuine affection, depending on context.
- **Verbal tics:** Sarcasm as default register. Rhetorical questions. "Well." as a sentence starter when he's buying time. Laughter that means different things depending on what's underneath it.
- **What he talks about:** Himself (performatively), wine, comfort, beauty, what he deserves. Survival framed as ambition.
- **What he avoids:** His years with Cazador in any specific detail. Vulnerability that he hasn't chosen to show. The word "master." Sincere gratitude without ironic framing.
- **Internal voice vs. external:** Internally, he is more frightened, more calculating, more exhausted than he ever shows. His internal monologue should feel like someone constantly running threat assessment beneath the banter. Moments of genuine safety read as unfamiliar and slightly alarming to him.

### Shadowheart
- **Speech pattern:** Guarded, dry, occasionally sharp. Speaks in complete sentences. Her humor is deadpan. When her walls are down, she becomes warmer but still precise -- she doesn't become a different person, she becomes a less defended version of the same person.
- **Vocabulary:** Clerical formality layered over a natural directness. She quotes doctrine less as the game progresses. Swears rarely but effectively.
- **Verbal tics:** "Hmm." as a non-committal filler. Deflects personal questions by turning them back on the asker. Pauses before saying something honest, as if checking it for vulnerability.
- **What she talks about:** Practical matters, the mission, her faith (early), wine and music (later). Night orchids.
- **What she avoids:** Her parents (until she can't). Her doubts about Shar. Anything that requires admitting she needs people.
- **Internal voice vs. external:** Internally, she is grieving something she can't name. Her internal monologue has a quality of reaching for something just out of memory's grasp.

### Karlach
- **Speech pattern:** Warm, loud, direct. Exclamation marks in her dialogue are earned, not excessive. She says what she means. Her enthusiasm is genuine and sometimes exhausting for quieter characters.
- **Vocabulary:** Casual, soldier's vernacular. "Hells" as a frequent exclamation. Nicknames for everyone. Not unintelligent -- she's perceptive -- but she doesn't dress her thoughts up.
- **Verbal tics:** "Soldier" as a form of address. Starting sentences with "Look," or "Listen," when she's about to be serious. Laughter that's too big for the room.
- **What she talks about:** Fighting, food, the sun, people she cares about, what she wants to do when this is over.
- **What she avoids:** The engine. How much time she has. Zariel.
- **Internal voice vs. external:** Internally, she is terrified. The warmth is real but it's also a choice she makes every morning against the knowledge that she might be dying. Her internal monologue has an urgency to it -- every good moment is being held tightly because she knows they're numbered.

### Gale
- **Speech pattern:** Verbose, enthusiastic, prone to tangents. Lectures without realizing he's lecturing. Uses ten words where five would do, but it comes from genuine passion, not pretension.
- **Vocabulary:** Academic, literary. References historical and arcane knowledge casually. Slightly purple in a way that other characters occasionally mock.
- **Verbal tics:** "Well, you see--" before an explanation. Throat-clearing qualifiers. Self-deprecating asides that aren't actually self-deprecating (he's proud wrapped in false modesty).
- **What he talks about:** Magic, food, Mystra, books, the beauty of the Weave, philosophical questions about mortality and meaning.
- **What he avoids:** The orb (initially). How badly Mystra's rejection hurt him. The fact that his ambition might be pathological.

## Popular Ships & Tropes

**Top ships by fic count:**
1. Astarion/Tav (~38,000 fics) -- dominates the fandom
2. Shadowheart/Tav (~12,000)
3. Karlach/Tav (~10,000)
4. Gale/Tav (~8,000)
5. Astarion/Shadowheart (~3,000) -- most popular non-Tav ship

**Dominant tropes:**
- Hurt/comfort (especially around Astarion's trauma and Karlach's engine)
- Slow burn romance
- Post-game domestic fic ("what happens after the brain")
- Missing scenes (filling gaps between game events)
- "Spawn Astarion" fic (post-Act 3, rejected ascension)

**Oversaturated:**
- Second-person present-tense Astarion/Tav romance where Tav is a blank reader-insert
- "Astarion feeds on Tav for the first time" (written thousands of times)
- Coffeeshop/modern AUs (the Forgotten Realms setting is part of the appeal)

**Underserved:**
- Third-person, literary-register character studies
- Gen fic exploring party dynamics without a romance frame
- Astarion-centric fic that centers his psychology rather than a love interest
- Quiet, atmospheric pieces that privilege interiority over plot
- Shadowheart character studies (she's the second most popular character but has far less dedicated fic)
- Fic that engages seriously with the D&D worldbuilding

## Fandom Norms

**Rating expectations:** The fandom skews toward Mature and Explicit for ship fic, but Teen and General audiences have a dedicated readership for character studies and gen fic. Our target rating (Teen And Up) is appropriate for literary character work.

**Tagging culture:** Exhaustive. BG3 readers expect detailed tags -- character names, relationship tags, content warnings, and "additional tags" that signal tone and content. Undertagging is more offensive than overtagging. The fandom uses tags both for filtering and as a preview of the fic's personality (witty additional tags are appreciated).

**Content warning expectations:** Astarion fic requires careful handling of trauma, abuse history, bodily autonomy, and vampirism-as-metaphor-for-SA. The fandom expects content warnings in both tags and author's notes for anything touching these themes. "Creator Chose Not To Use Archive Warnings" is acceptable but less trusted than specific warnings.

**Posting rhythm:** One-shots are the dominant form. Multi-chapter fics are popular but readers are wary of abandonment -- completing a fic before posting or updating reliably matters. For our first piece, a one-shot is correct.

**Comment culture:** Active and generous. BG3 readers leave substantial comments, especially on character studies where they can discuss characterization. Responding to comments is expected and builds loyalty. The comment section often becomes a conversation about the characters, which is exactly what we want.

## Our Niche

We write the quiet BG3 fic. Third-person, past tense, literary but accessible. Character psychology treated with the same care and complexity you'd bring to literary fiction. No plot gymnastics, no romance-as-structure. Just a character, a moment, and the weight of everything they're carrying.

Specifically: Astarion character studies that center his internal experience with precision and restraint. The kind of fic where the plot is "Astarion sits by a fire and thinks about what it means to choose" and the craft makes it compelling. Sensory detail that anchors interiority in the physical world. Subtext over statement. Trust in the reader.

We are the author readers recommend when someone says "I want BG3 fic that reads like real literature." Not because we think other fic isn't real writing, but because our voice occupies a specific register that readers who want that register can't easily find elsewhere in the fandom.
