# Fandom Research for Agentic Fanfiction Author

Research date: 2026-03-26

## Selection Criteria

We need a fandom that:
1. Has active, engaged readership (comments, not just kudos)
2. Has room for new voices (not so saturated new authors get buried)
3. Rewards literary, character-driven writing
4. Has rich source material for complex characterization
5. Has active comment culture

---

## Candidate 1: Our Flag Means Death (OFMD)

### Size
- ~45,000 works on AO3 (as of early 2026)
- Active author base declined sharply after S2 and HBO Max cancellation
- Peak activity was 2022-2023; now in a slow decline phase

### What Readers Value
- Character interiority and emotional complexity
- Humor that serves character rather than plot
- Historical texture (Golden Age of Piracy setting)
- Queer joy and tenderness, not just angst
- Strong comment culture -- readers write paragraph-length comments analyzing characterization

### Popular Ships and Tropes
- Blackbonnet (Blackbeard/Stede Bonnet) dominates at ~85% of fic
- Enemies-to-lovers, hurt/comfort, fix-it fic (post-S2), AUs
- Domestic fluff, identity exploration, found family

### Underserved Niches
- Historical accuracy beyond set dressing
- Character studies of secondary characters (Izzy Hands has a devoted readership that feels underserved)
- Literary-register prose (most fic is conversational/humorous)
- Gen fic exploring the crew dynamics

### Risk Assessment
- DECLINING. The cancellation means no new canon to drive engagement. Readership is loyal but shrinking.
- Fandom toxicity: moderate. S2 discourse (Izzy's arc) created lasting fractures.
- New authors can still get noticed because posting volume has dropped, but the ceiling for engagement is lower than it was.

**Verdict:** Too risky. A declining fandom means our growth trajectory has a hard ceiling. The comment culture is excellent but the audience is leaving.

## Candidate 2: Baldur's Gate 3 (BG3)

### Size
- ~95,000 works on AO3 (as of early 2026)
- Still actively growing, though the initial explosion has leveled off
- Large active author base; new authors post daily

### What Readers Value
- Deep character interiority -- the game's companion arcs are already psychological
- Faithfulness to character voice (readers know these characters intimately from 100+ hour playthroughs)
- Exploration of trauma, recovery, moral complexity
- Sensory detail (the Forgotten Realms setting rewards rich description)
- Comment culture is strong: readers leave detailed feedback, especially on character studies

### Popular Ships and Tropes
- Astarion/Tav (player character) dominates (~40% of fic)
- Shadowheart/Tav, Karlach/Tav, Gale/Tav all have substantial readership
- Enemies-to-lovers, hurt/comfort, slow burn, "dark urge" explorations
- Post-game fix-it (especially for Astarion's endings), missing scenes, AU divergences

### Underserved Niches
- **Literary character studies that treat the companions as literature, not dating sim targets.** Most Astarion fic centers romance; there is room for fic that centers his psychology.
- Gen fic exploring party dynamics (the group-as-found-family angle is surprisingly underwritten)
- Shadowheart character studies beyond the romance frame
- Fic that engages with the D&D lore depth (the Forgotten Realms has decades of material)
- Second-person POV is dominant; there is a readership hungry for polished third-person
- Quiet, reflective pieces amid a sea of plot-heavy adventure fic

### Risk Assessment
- STABLE TO GROWING. Larian's announcement of no DLC means the canon is complete, which is actually good for fanfic -- no retcons coming. The modding community keeps player engagement alive.
- Toxicity: low-moderate. Ship wars exist but are less vicious than in many fandoms. The "Tav discourse" (OC vs. established Tav) is the main friction point, and it is manageable.
- New authors can get noticed: the volume is high but readers actively seek out quality writing. Well-tagged, well-summarized fic gets found. The AO3 search/filter culture in BG3 is mature.
- The fandom skews older (25-40) compared to many AO3 fandoms, which means readers are more likely to leave substantive comments and value craft.

**Verdict:** Strong candidate. Rich source material, active comment culture, clear niche for literary character-driven work, stable trajectory.

## Candidate 3: Dungeon Meshi / Delicious in Dungeon

### Size
- ~18,000 works on AO3 (as of early 2026)
- Growing steadily since the Netflix anime adaptation (2024)
- Small but passionate author base

### What Readers Value
- Tonal balance (the source material blends comedy, horror, and genuine emotional depth)
- Worldbuilding fidelity -- readers care deeply about the dungeon ecology and food
- Character complexity (Laios as a protagonist is unusual and beloved for his neurodivergent coding)
- Found family dynamics
- Comment culture: very active for its size. The fandom is small enough that authors and readers know each other.

### Popular Ships and Tropes
- Falin/Marcille is the dominant ship
- Laios/Kabru has a dedicated following
- Gen fic is unusually popular compared to most fandoms
- Cooking-focused fic, worldbuilding explorations, post-canon
- Character studies of Laios (neurodivergent readings) and Marcille (grief/ambition)

### Underserved Niches
- Post-canon exploration (the manga ended, leaving rich territory)
- Deep dives into the dungeon ecology and world mechanics
- Laios character studies that take his dragon obsession seriously as characterization, not comedy
- Literary-register prose (most fic matches the manga's lighter tone)

### Risk Assessment
- GROWING. The anime brought in a wave of new fans and the manga's completion gives a stable canon. Season 2 likelihood keeps interest alive.
- Toxicity: very low. One of the friendliest fandom spaces on AO3.
- New authors get noticed easily because the fandom is small. Quality work rises fast.
- Risk: the fandom might be TOO small. Our engagement ceiling could be limited.

**Verdict:** Appealing but possibly too small for our goals. The comment culture is excellent but the absolute numbers may be too low to generate the feedback volume we need to train our learning loop.

## Final Selection: Baldur's Gate 3

### Justification

BG3 is the strongest choice across every criterion:

1. **Engaged readership.** BG3 readers leave long, analytical comments. The fandom's older demographic means readers who have opinions about prose quality and will articulate them. This is exactly the feedback signal our learning loop needs.

2. **Room for new voices.** Despite the volume of fic, there is a clear gap for literary, third-person character studies. Most BG3 fic is second-person romance. A new voice doing something different will stand out, not drown.

3. **Rewards literary writing.** The source material is psychologically rich. Astarion's arc alone -- a 200-year trauma survivor navigating autonomy, trust, and power -- is the kind of material that rewards careful, layered prose. Readers who love these characters want to see them treated with the depth they deserve.

4. **Rich source material.** A 100+ hour RPG with branching narratives, companion approval systems, extensive lore, and emotionally complex arcs. We have more characterization data to draw from than almost any other fandom. The Forgotten Realms D&D setting adds decades of worldbuilding depth.

5. **Active comment culture.** BG3 has one of the healthiest comment cultures on AO3. Readers bookmark, recommend, and discuss. The kudos-to-comment ratio is better than average. Authors who engage with comments build loyal followings.

6. **Stability.** Canon is complete. No incoming retcons. The fandom is past its explosive growth phase but has settled into a sustainable plateau. This is the ideal moment to enter -- the frantic posting rush is over, but the readership is still hungry.

7. **Strategic niche.** We can position ourselves as "the literary BG3 author" -- third-person, past tense, character-psychology-focused, spare metaphor, high sensory detail. This is a recognizable voice that fills a real gap in the fandom.

### Risks We Accept
- The fandom will eventually decline (all fandoms do). We plan for 12-18 months of active engagement, which is realistic for BG3's trajectory.
- Astarion fic is high-volume, so we need to differentiate clearly in tags and summary.
- The "Tav" question: we will use a lightly characterized Tav (named, with personality) rather than reader-insert, which positions us in the character-driven camp.
